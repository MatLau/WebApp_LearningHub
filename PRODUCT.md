# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Personale ATA (segreterie scolastiche)** — administrative staff who operate SIDI, NoiPA, Pago In Rete, and school management systems (Argo, Spaggiari, Nuvola). They use the app to understand where AI can assist them in the communicative and documentational layer of their work, without confusing it with the operational gestionali.

**Docenti** — teachers focused on pedagogy, responsible student use of AI, inclusion (BES), and risk assessment. They use the app to build a working mental model of AI in education.

Both groups are non-technical professionals in an Italian public-sector school context. They arrive with varying levels of digital confidence and limited time. The training is professional development, not leisure learning.

## Product Purpose

A gamified e-learning platform that teaches Italian school personnel (ATA staff and teachers) to use AI responsibly — within GDPR and EU AI Act compliance — in their daily institutional work. Success means a learner completes all relevant modules, passes module quizzes (≥60%), accumulates XP and badges, and earns a printable certificate they can attach to their professional development record.

## Brand Personality

Professionale, chiaro, motivante.

The voice is that of a knowledgeable colleague, not a textbook. It takes the content seriously (GDPR, AI Act, MIM guidelines) while keeping the learner engaged enough to finish. No condescension; no oversimplification.

## Anti-references

- **Consumer minimalism (iOS Settings aesthetic)** — too casual, loses the authoritative frame of an official training program. Learners need to feel they are doing something that counts.
- **Generic LMS feel** — Moodle-style table-heavy layouts signal "mandatory bureaucratic checkbox." The gamification layer only works if the interface feels earned.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibilità senza rigidità.** The UI must read as institutional-quality without being as stiff as a PA portal. Authority comes from clarity and consistency, not formalism.
2. **Progressione sempre visibile.** XP bars, module completion states, and badge systems are load-bearing motivational affordances — never decorative. Every screen should tell the learner where they stand and what comes next.
3. **Contenuto prima dell'estetica.** Module content is dense (normative, procedural, compliance-heavy). The design must guarantee comprehension: line length, contrast, typographic hierarchy all serve the reader first.
4. **Basso carico cognitivo.** Users are often context-switching between this app and their actual work tools. The interface should require no orientation time — navigation states and progress signals must be unambiguous.
5. **Distinto dai gestionali.** The app shares a user base with SIDI, NoiPA, and Argo but must feel clearly different: warmer, more human, goal-oriented rather than form-driven.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced-motion preference respected (already implemented via `prefers-reduced-motion` media query). Color choices must maintain sufficient contrast in both light and dark modes for users with limited color discrimination.
